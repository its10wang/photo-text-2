Demographic

Two Bridges is a tiny neighborhood in lower Manhattan sandwiched between the Manhattan Bridge and Brooklyn Bridge. Most New Yorkers have never heard of . But being under-the-radar is just the way its residents like it. Unlike some of its neighbors, it hasn't been gentrified yet, leaving an old school, affordable neighborhood of long-time residents and brick walk-ups (though new development is creeping in). There is also a large public housing development in the  neighborhood. 

 

Two Bridges was historically an Irish and Italian neighborhood, but after the war and the construction of public housing high rises in the 1950s, the area became more diverse with the arrival of black and Hispanic residents. Two Bridges has recently been populated by first- and second-generation Chinese immigrants. 

Two Bridges had a lower percentage of white residents in 2010, with less than a quarter of the population identifying as white. Approximately 13.3 percent identified as black or African American, a significant 24.4 percent increase since 2000. The Chinese population was the largest in the neighborhood, accounting for more than 44 percent of the total population. Around 28% of the population identified as Hispanic, with Puerto Ricans accounting for 71.6 percent of the Hispanic population. According to the most recent Census data, foreign-born residents made up 44.7 percent of the Two Bridge population. A significant proportion of foreign-born residents (83 percent) emigrated from Asia, while another 13 percent emigrated from Latin America. The neighborhood continues to be home to low- to moderate-income families and has a reputation for being gritty. 

 Richard Fortus, guitarist for Guns N' Roses and a Two Bridges resident, described the neighborhood as "the only neighborhood left in Manhattan that doesn't have a Starbucks.".
